About me

I am a experienced graphic designer and Front-end Developer based in Bergen, Norway, with over five decades of life experiences shaping my creative journey. Currently, I'm immersed in the world of freelancing, where I wear the hats of a graphic designer and web designer. Simultaneously, I'm pursuing my studies at Noroff School of Technology and Digital Media, further fueling my passion for innovation.

The decision to delve into Front-end Development at Noroff was pivotal. It was here that I discovered the potent fusion of design and coding, unlocking a new dimension of creativity and potential. In the domain of technology, women can, will, and unquestionably do leave a lasting impact on the landscape of innovation and digital transformation - regardless of age and experience. I proudly count myself among these trailblazers, working with various on the www for over 30 years, and still counting.

Throughout my professional journey, I've deliberately eschewed the idea of settling into a single career path. Instead, I've embraced the diverse opportunities that have come my way. I've come to see my indecision not as a weakness but as a wellspring of curiosity, engagement, and a relentless commitment to seeing every endeavor through to fruition.

Fortunately, my chosen fields of design and development are ever-evolving. This fluidity allows me to effortlessly traverse the boundaries between the physical and digital realms. It empowers me to leverage my skills and adapt to emerging trends, thereby shaping innovative solutions that stand the test of time.

At my core, I harbor an unwavering passion for turning imaginative concepts into tangible reality. I relish the dynamic nature of the creative and digital landscape, where each day presents fresh challenges and opportunities. This mindset propels me to explore the exciting possibilities that arise at the intersection of the physical and the digital, and I am poised to continue making a meaningful mark in this ever-evolving domain.
